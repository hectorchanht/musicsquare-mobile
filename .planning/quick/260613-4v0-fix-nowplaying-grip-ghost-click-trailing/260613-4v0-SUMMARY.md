# Phase quick-260613-4v0 Plan 01: Fix NowPlaying Grip Ghost-Click Summary

One-liner: A window-level, capture-phase, one-shot trailing-click suppressor in NowPlaying.svelte stops a grip TAP's browser compatibility click from landing on the play toggle or seek slider after the sheet moves.

## What Was Built

A grip TAP synchronously moves the sheet (`sheetState` reassignment / `selectTab`), and the browser's trailing compatibility click â€” fired after `pointerup` at the tap coordinates â€” lands on whatever element now sits under the finger (`.play` onclick = play/pause, or `.track` onclick={seek} = ~15-25s seek-back). The fix swallows that one trailing click.

Implementation (Task 1, commit `267728b`):

1. `gripSuppressTimer` handle declared alongside `snapTimer`.
2. Three functions added above `gripDown`, mirroring `coverSwipe.ts` (lines 89-118 / 188-200):
   - `disarmGripClickSuppressor()` â€” removes the window click listener + clears/null the timer.
   - `suppressGripClick(e)` â€” `stopPropagation()` + `preventDefault()` + disarm.
   - `armGripClickSuppressor()` â€” disarm-stale, then `window.addEventListener('click', suppressGripClick, true)` + 350ms self-expiry (WR-05: touch taps often emit no trailing click).
3. `disarmGripClickSuppressor()` called at the top of `gripDown` (stale-suppressor cleanup on a new gesture).
4. `armGripClickSuppressor()` called in the two `gripUp` tap branches that MOVE the sheet:
   - `gripStartTab` sub-branch (prevents tab-button onclick double-fire + ghost click on closedâ†’half).
   - generic grip-toggle sub-branch (the reported bug path).
   - The `gripStartPlainButton` sub-branch is left unchanged (WR-02 â€” Clear-queue-style buttons need their own onclick).
5. Removed the now-dead grip `onclick={(e) => { if (gripActive) {...} }}` guard from the `.grip` markup â€” `gripActive` is always false by click time and the click never targets the grip.

### Load-bearing deviation from coverSwipe.ts

`coverSwipe` arms the suppressor on the gesture NODE because `setPointerCapture` retargets the trailing click to that node. The grip case differs: the grip never captures on a TAP (only on a â‰Ą8px drag), and the ghost click targets the unrelated element that slid under the finger. The listener therefore MUST be at WINDOW level, CAPTURE phase, to intercept the click before it reaches its real target. This is documented in the source comment.

Drag (â‰Ą8px) and flick paths were left as-is: drags `setPointerCapture` (retargeting the trailing mouse click to the grip) and the snap is deferred 290ms â€” not part of this bug.
